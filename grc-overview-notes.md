# GRC Overview Notes

## What is GRC?

GRC stands for Governance, Risk, and Compliance. It is a business approach that helps organizations manage security, reduce risks, and comply with laws, regulations, and standards.

---

## Governance

Governance refers to the policies, procedures, and decision-making processes that guide an organization.

Examples:
- Password Policy
- Access Control Policy
- Remote Work Policy

Purpose:
To ensure employees follow approved rules and processes.

---

## Risk

Risk is the possibility of an event causing harm to the organization.

Examples:
- Data breach
- Malware infection
- Insider threat
- Lost company laptop

Purpose:
To identify and reduce threats before they become incidents.

---

## Compliance

Compliance means following applicable laws, regulations, standards, and company requirements.

Examples:
- ISO 27001
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- Internal Security Policies

Purpose:
To avoid penalties, reduce risk, and maintain trust.

---

## Why GRC Matters

Benefits:
- Reduces business risk
- Improves security posture
- Supports regulatory compliance
- Protects company reputation
- Helps organizations operate efficiently

---

## GRC Analyst Responsibilities

- Perform risk assessments
- Support audits
- Review compliance requirements
- Maintain risk registers
- Update policies and procedures
- Create reports for management
- Coordinate with IT and business teams

---

## Summary

GRC helps organizations make better decisions, manage risks, and comply with required standards and regulations.
